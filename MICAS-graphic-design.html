MICAS
Summer Celebration

Event branding, poster and invitation design — Adobe Photoshop, Illustrator & InDesign — 2019
Brief:

The MICAS Summer Celebration, held in the summer of 2019, consisted of a talk by curator Edith Devaney (Artists & their Archives) and a video projection of JUMPSTART, a projection of the history of Maltese contemporary art.

Challenges:

1. The projection was created externally and finalised at the last minute, meaning there were no visuals to align with. Conseqently, it made unclear which activity was the focus of the event.



2. Using colour. MICAS stakeholders were often reluctant to use colour in event visuals, but given the title, it seemed remiss not to incorporate some colour in the design.



3. Communicating the concept of the event while mainting consistency with the MICAS brand, especially as JUMPSTART was an outside project, albeit developed by MICAS stakeholders.

The MICAS branding guidelines were made by the same design agency that designed the MICAS logo. They defined six colours as part of these branding guidelines, along with two typefaces: Euclid Flex and Arial.


Despite the branding colours, MICAS stakeholders were regularly reluctant to use colour in event visuals—given the event's name (Summer Celebration!) I wanted to find a compromise.


Through the preliminary experiments I wanted to establish how I could use colour. Despite the results being inadequate, these helped me establish what colours to use and that Euclid Flex might not be a suitable typeface for this project.





The most pertinent information was the branding and the date, and these came together as simple lower-case text. A lower-case font in the lighter orange were consistent with the simple designs of previous events, but I found the Euclid Flex problematic.


Euclid Flex has been tricky to work with for me. A substitue I had used previously is HK Grotesk, which complements the MICAS branding well and complements the 'summery-ness' of the orange.






The JUMPSTART project was undertaken by academic stakeholders of MICAS to build an archive of Contemporary Art history in Malta, which meant I had access to a lot of images.


These images constituted the visual elements of the timeline projection itself and the prima materia of the project—since I did not have access to any visuals of the JUMPSTART projection I couldn't align my visuals with it, and incoroporating the archive images was the safest way forward.


The answer to incorporating the images in the design proved straightforward but it took me a while to get to it. This 'timeline' design of the iamges proved an effective way to link the idea of the timeline projection and the 'archival' nature of the project, was liked by the stakeholders, and importantly, worked well when incorporated within the designs produced for print.
Final designs for poster and invitation prints for MICAS Summer Celebration.

PS: MICAS hires a design agency for most of its design needs. As an only in-house designer, more often than not, my role is to fix any fix brief shortcomings, missing visuals from the agency, or on-the-fly needs, and usualy have to adhere to existing designs, and without any guidance from the agency (it falls outside their remit). This is the only project that I designed in full at MICAS—sadly, after 4 years, I realised the role has not been conducive to me developing good design practices or a consistent portfolio, albeit having a lot of freedom to learn and experiment.
